# AI use

This project may be developed with AI assistance.

AI assistance can be used for brainstorming, drafting, structuring, coding support, refactoring suggestions, documentation, and testing ideas.

However, AI is not treated as an authority.

## Principles

- AI may assist, but humans remain responsible for decisions.
- AI-generated suggestions should be reviewed before they are accepted.
- The project should not hide substantial AI assistance when it matters for authorship, trust, or interpretation.
- Open, inspectable, and locally understandable structures are preferred where possible.
- The project should remain critical of AI-related power concentration, resource use, data extraction, black-box dependency, and the social effects of automation.

A working formula for the project:

> AI-assisted, human-shaped, open to review.

## Why this matters

The project explores AI as an ambivalent tool: it may support communication, accessibility, translation, learning, and creativity, but it may also concentrate power, obscure responsibility, and reshape social worlds in one-sided ways.

A Nexus should therefore not celebrate AI uncritically. It should help make questions of power, responsibility, openness, and human response-ability visible.
