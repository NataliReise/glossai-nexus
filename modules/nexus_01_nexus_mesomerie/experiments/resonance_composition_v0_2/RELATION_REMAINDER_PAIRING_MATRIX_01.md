# Relation–Remainder Pairing Matrix 01

## Status

This document records a provisional poetic review of relation–remainder pairings in the current `mixed-threshold-return-world` library.

It does not change block weights, composer logic, compatibility rules, routes, or runtime behaviour.

Its purpose is to identify which pairings may be used freely, which should remain marked or rare, and where a deliberate emphatic reprise may be poetically useful without becoming a recognisable machine stamp.

The review follows these principles:

> Signature strength is not a quality score.

> Anti-formula quality must be carried by the form of the Nexus, not by a central memory of its copies.

> A Nexus should remain lean enough to understand and rich enough to resonate.

The recommended pair weights below are provisional review values only. They are not yet implemented metadata.

---

## Pairing classes

```text
freely compatible
→ suitable for regular use; the two lines support one another without excessive self-repetition

compatible but marked
→ poetically valid, but noticeably dense, repetitive, or conceptually explicit

rare pairing
→ strong but highly recognisable; should remain available without becoming a normal route

emphatic reprise
→ deliberate repetition that may function like a refrain when used sparingly

avoid pairing
→ reserved for combinations that would be mechanically or poetically counterproductive
```

No current pairing is classified as `avoid pairing`.

# 2. Detailed pairing review

## 2.1 `relation.interval.01`

```text
Two returns hold one interval open
```

This relation is one of the strongest formulations in the current prototype. It expresses two returns, continued separateness, and a shared open interval in a very compact form. Its poetic strength also gives it very high signature strength.

### With `remainder.passage.01`

```text
Two returns hold one interval open
{return_word} remains where the passage stays open
```

- **Pairing class:** rare pairing
- **Signature strength:** very high
- **Poetic function:** sustained openness
- **Recommended pair weight:** 1

The relation opens an interval; the remainder keeps a passage open and places the return word there. The sequence is coherent and strongly Nexus-like, but `open` and `stays open` create an immediate conceptual reprise. Both lines also use abstract spatial language. The pairing should remain available as a concentrated Nexus formulation, but not become a normal ending path.

### With `remainder.water.02`

```text
Two returns hold one interval open
{return_word} waits in the water between them
```

- **Pairing class:** freely compatible
- **Signature strength:** medium-high
- **Poetic function:** embodiment of the interval
- **Recommended pair weight:** 3

The abstract interval becomes a sensory image. `Between them` carries the relational space forward without repeating `open`. The water remainder softens the conceptual density of the relation and makes this a strong regular pairing.

### With `remainder.direction.03`

```text
Two returns hold one interval open
{return_word} keeps the shared direction visible
```

- **Pairing class:** compatible but marked
- **Signature strength:** high
- **Poetic function:** interval becoming orientation
- **Recommended pair weight:** 2

The pairing develops from an open relational space toward a visible shared orientation. It is not merely repetitive, but both lines are conceptually dense and state central Nexus principles explicitly. It should occur less often than the water pairing.

## 2.2 `relation.direction.02`

```text
Their separate currents share one direction
```

This relation states `convergence without fusion` directly: the currents remain separate while gaining a shared orientation.

### With `remainder.passage.01`

```text
Their separate currents share one direction
{return_word} remains where the passage stays open
```

- **Pairing class:** freely compatible
- **Signature strength:** medium-high
- **Poetic function:** shared orientation opening continuation
- **Recommended pair weight:** 3

The first line establishes direction; the second shifts into an open spatial continuation. The images do not duplicate one another, and the return word remains in a possible passage rather than being absorbed into the current.

### With `remainder.water.02`

```text
Their separate currents share one direction
{return_word} waits in the water between them
```

- **Pairing class:** freely compatible
- **Signature strength:** medium
- **Poetic function:** preserving the relational space
- **Recommended pair weight:** 3

The water image belongs naturally to the current motif while redirecting attention from shared orientation to the space between the currents. The return word remains a separate trace. This is one of the softer and less programmatic pairings.

### With `remainder.direction.03`

```text
Their separate currents share one direction
{return_word} keeps the shared direction visible
```

- **Pairing class:** emphatic reprise
- **Signature strength:** very high
- **Poetic function:** naming and preserving shared orientation
- **Recommended pair weight:** 1

`Share one direction` is immediately resumed as `the shared direction visible`. The second line gives the return word an active preserving role, so the repetition is not empty. Used rarely, it may work as a deliberate refrain. Used often, it would reveal the assembly pattern quickly.

## 2.3 `relation.shore.03`

```text
One shore becomes visible between them
```

This relation is more image-led than the interval and direction relations. A possible shared place becomes visible without requiring arrival or fusion.

### With `remainder.passage.01`

```text
One shore becomes visible between them
{return_word} remains where the passage stays open
```

- **Pairing class:** freely compatible
- **Signature strength:** medium-high
- **Poetic function:** visible arrival without closure
- **Recommended pair weight:** 3

The visible shore suggests a possible place of arrival; the open passage prevents that arrival from becoming final closure. The relation remains concrete while the remainder keeps the movement available.

### With `remainder.water.02`

```text
One shore becomes visible between them
{return_word} waits in the water between them
```

- **Pairing class:** compatible but marked
- **Signature strength:** high
- **Poetic function:** visible destination with suspended arrival
- **Recommended pair weight:** 2

The image is highly organic: the shore is visible while the return word still waits in the water. However, the direct repetition of `between them` is strongly audible. This may work in a suitable poem, but should not be a default pairing.

### With `remainder.direction.03`

```text
One shore becomes visible between them
{return_word} keeps the shared direction visible
```

- **Pairing class:** rare pairing
- **Signature strength:** high
- **Poetic function:** destination becoming orientation
- **Recommended pair weight:** 1

The visible shore becomes a visible shared direction. The development is intelligible, but `visible` repeats immediately and the two lines partly restate the same recognition in different conceptual forms. The pairing remains valid but should be rare.

## 2.4 `relation.distance.04`

```text
Distance begins to answer without closing
```

This relation is both strong and delicate. It creates response across distance while explicitly preserving that distance. It also closely echoes the current wish-entry formulation `distance begins to answer`, which already makes it a candidate for static rarity.

### With `remainder.passage.01`

```text
Distance begins to answer without closing
{return_word} remains where the passage stays open
```

- **Pairing class:** rare pairing
- **Signature strength:** very high
- **Poetic function:** answer without closure
- **Recommended pair weight:** 1

Both lines maintain openness and refuse closure. The sequence is philosophically coherent, but `without closing` and `stays open` nearly restate one another. The pairing is a concentrated project formulation and should remain rare.

### With `remainder.water.02`

```text
Distance begins to answer without closing
{return_word} waits in the water between them
```

- **Pairing class:** freely compatible
- **Signature strength:** medium-high
- **Poetic function:** embodying responsive distance
- **Recommended pair weight:** 3

The water remainder translates the abstract answering distance into an image. The continued separation is shown rather than repeated. This is the strongest regular partner for the distance relation, although whole-poem compatibility should later watch for repeated `waits` or repeated `distance begins to answer` elsewhere in the route.

### With `remainder.direction.03`

```text
Distance begins to answer without closing
{return_word} keeps the shared direction visible
```

- **Pairing class:** compatible but marked
- **Signature strength:** very high
- **Poetic function:** responsive distance becoming orientation
- **Recommended pair weight:** 1

The development from response across distance to a preserved shared orientation is meaningful, but both lines are conceptually dense and highly characteristic of the current world. The pairing should remain possible without becoming frequent.

# 3. Provisional synthesis

## Water as poetic de-densification

The water remainder often works as a poetic de-densifier. It translates abstract relations such as `interval` or `distance` into a sensory relational image rather than explaining them again.

This does not mean that water should dominate the whole library. The current world already contains many water motifs. The finding applies only to the present relation–remainder surface.

## Passage and openness

The passage remainder works well when the relation introduces a different primary operation, such as shared direction or visible shore.

It becomes highly marked when the relation already centres on openness or non-closing, because the ending then repeats the same conceptual operation.

## Direction as a high-signature ending

The direction remainder is the most conceptually explicit and signature-bearing current ending. It should generally remain less frequent, especially beside relations already containing `direction`, `visible`, or another strongly stated Nexus principle.

## Repetition may remain intentional

Immediate repetition is not automatically an error. `direction + direction` is retained as an `emphatic reprise` because the second line gives the return word a preserving role.

The relevant distinction is:

```text
intentional poetic reprise
≠
accidental template exposure
```

# 4. Implementation boundary

This matrix does not yet decide how pair weights should interact with existing block weights.

Possible later models include:

```text
relation block weight
× remainder block weight
× pair weight
```

or a simpler pair-level selection model.

No implementation choice should be made until:

1. the review has been accepted,
2. additional relation and remainder alternatives have been considered,
3. seeded probe corpora have been compared,
4. the simplest adequate runtime representation has been identified.

The preferred principle remains:

> So much structure as necessary, so little complexity as possible.

---

# 5. Recommended next steps

1. Keep the current production and V0.1 logic unchanged.
2. Treat the matrix as review data, not runtime metadata.
3. Curate new relation and remainder alternatives from different poetic operations.
4. Prioritise alternatives based on listening, temporal delay, shared rhythm, remaining warmth, sound across distance, presence without naming, and arrival without closure.
5. Avoid creating mere synonyms for `open`, `direction`, `visible`, `distance`, `water`, or `return`.
6. After new alternatives exist, decide whether pair weights are still necessary at runtime.
7. Only then create a new seeded probe series and compare both frequency and poetic effect.
