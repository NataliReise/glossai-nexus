# Signature Strength Review 01

## Status

This document records a provisional poetic review of the current `mixed-threshold-return-world` library.

It does not change block weights, routes, compatibility rules, or runtime behaviour.

Its purpose is to identify where a recognisable generator stamp may arise before any anti-formula implementation is added.

The review follows two project principles:

> Signature strength is not a quality score.

and:

> A Nexus should remain lean enough to understand and rich enough to resonate.

Therefore the first response to a high-signature line should usually be better library curation, not more composer logic.

## Review question

For each line or pairing, the central question is:

> How strongly would repeated use make the generator recognisable?

This is different from asking whether the line is poetically successful.

A strong line may receive a high signature rating precisely because it is memorable.

## Provisional scale

```text
low
→ supportive, flexible, unlikely to reveal the generator by itself

medium
→ recognisable in repeated use, but adaptable across several contexts

high
→ memorable, structurally central, or likely to expose a recurring assembly pattern
```

The ratings remain provisional until they are compared against larger seeded corpora.

# 1. Long-form block inventory

## Field blocks

### `field.threshold.01`

```text
At the open edge of night
```

**Signature strength:** medium

**Why:** The line is vivid and compact, but still broad enough to support different following movements. Repeated use would be noticeable because `open edge of night` is a distinctive phrase.

**Main risk:** clustering with other `open` and threshold formulations.

**Alternative need:** moderate. One or two equally strong field openings from a different image family would help.

### `field.frame.02`

```text
An open frame lets distance enter
```

**Signature strength:** high

**Why:** The syntax and conceptual movement are both distinctive. `open frame`, `distance`, and `enter` make the line highly characteristic of this world.

**Main risk:** repeated `open + distance + entering` architecture across field, wish, relation, and Echo.

**Alternative need:** high. This function should eventually have at least one less abstract and one less explicitly spatial alternative.

### `field.window.03`

```text
The window keeps two directions visible
```

**Signature strength:** high

**Why:** This line states a core project relation directly: two directions remain visible. It is poetically useful but conceptually central enough to become a machine signature quickly.

**Main risk:** reinforcement by `shared direction visible` in the remainder or Echo.

**Alternative need:** high. A second visibility field without `direction` would reduce conceptual repetition.

## Atmosphere blocks

### `atmosphere.rain.01`

```text
Summer rain softens the threshold
```

**Signature strength:** high

**Why:** This is one of the most memorable and reusable lines in the current library. Its strength makes recurrence conspicuous.

**Main risk:** becoming the default atmospheric stamp of the whole module.

**Alternative need:** high. Additional atmosphere blocks should not merely substitute another weather word into the same syntax.

### `atmosphere.trace.02`

```text
Rain leaves a silver trace across the floor
```

**Signature strength:** high

**Why:** The image is concrete and polished. Repetition of `rain`, `trace`, and `across` makes it strongly identifiable.

**Main risk:** clustering with the same-word proxy `the first trace` and other trace-derived Echo lines.

**Alternative need:** high. At least one atmosphere should avoid both rain and trace.

### `atmosphere.nearness.03`

```text
Wet air carries the nearness of encounter
```

**Signature strength:** medium-high

**Why:** It is tonally rich and less visually obvious, but the phrase `nearness of encounter` is unusual enough to become recognisable.

**Main risk:** conceptual abstraction appearing beside other abstract relation lines.

**Alternative need:** moderate. The line already broadens the sensory field and should probably remain comparatively rare.

## Wish-entry blocks

### `wish.frame.01`

```text
{wish_word} crosses the open frame
```

**Signature strength:** medium

**Why:** The structure is clear and flexible, but `crosses the open frame` strongly repeats the current threshold vocabulary.

**Main risk:** field and wish both using `open frame` in the same poem.

**Alternative need:** moderate. Its function is useful, but more syntax families would help.

### `wish.distance.02`

```text
{wish_word} waits where distance begins to answer
```

**Signature strength:** high

**Why:** `distance begins to answer` is a strong and memorable conceptual phrase. It also reappears almost exactly in a relation block and Echo candidate.

**Main risk:** accidental self-quotation across wish, relation, and Echo.

**Alternative need:** high. The answering function should receive at least one differently phrased variant.

### `wish.rain-room.03`

```text
Rain leaves room for {wish_word}
```

**Signature strength:** medium-high

**Why:** It is simple and effective, but the syntax `Rain leaves ...` can strongly echo `Rain leaves a silver trace ...`.

**Main risk:** incantatory repetition becoming a default pattern rather than a curated effect.

**Alternative need:** moderate-high. Keep the line, but add another making-room construction without rain.

### `wish.light.04`

```text
{wish_word} follows the light beyond the glass
```

**Signature strength:** medium-high

**Why:** The line is graceful and flexible, but `light`, `glass`, and outward following create a strong tonal marker.

**Main risk:** overconcentration of light and visibility language.

**Alternative need:** moderate. It benefits from a companion movement based on sound, texture, or time rather than light.

## Movement blocks

### `movement.tide.01`

```text
The tide gathers its scattered roads
```

**Signature strength:** high

**Why:** This is a striking metaphor and therefore highly memorable.

**Main risk:** becoming a signature image that appears too often across otherwise different poems.

**Alternative need:** high. The gathering movement should have another image family.

### `movement.shore.02`

```text
Returning water folds the shore inward
```

**Signature strength:** medium-high

**Why:** Strong image, but less conceptually explanatory than some relation lines.

**Main risk:** repeated water/shore folding beside shore-based relation and remainder structures.

**Alternative need:** moderate.

### `movement.departure.03`

```text
The tide turns back without undoing departure
```

**Signature strength:** high

**Why:** It states one of the central ethics of the world in a memorable formulation: return does not erase departure.

**Main risk:** the phrase `without undoing` also appears in an Echo candidate, making the system visibly recursive.

**Alternative need:** high. Preserve the idea, but provide another construction with different syntax and imagery.

## Response-movement blocks

### `response.current.01`

```text
A narrow current answers from another direction
```

**Signature strength:** medium-high

**Why:** The line supports reciprocity well, but `current`, `answers`, and `another direction` overlap strongly with the dominant motif families.

**Main risk:** direction language accumulating across response, relation, and remainder.

**Alternative need:** moderate-high.

### `response.path.02`

```text
A stream returns by its own path
```

**Signature strength:** medium

**Why:** Clear, concrete, and comparatively flexible. It preserves separateness without overexplaining it.

**Main risk:** recurrence of `return` and `path`, but the syntax is less conspicuous than other core lines.

**Alternative need:** moderate-low.

### `response.seaward.03`

```text
Another current bends seaward through the dark
```

**Signature strength:** medium

**Why:** Visually specific and less doctrinal. It contributes useful tonal variation.

**Main risk:** `another current` repeats the current family, but the movement itself is distinctive.

**Alternative need:** moderate-low.

## Relation blocks

### `relation.interval.01`

```text
Two returns hold one interval open
```

**Signature strength:** very high

**Why:** This is one of the central formulations of the whole prototype. It is concise, unusual, and structurally powerful.

**Main risk:** becoming the most obvious poetic machine stamp, especially because it may also appear verbatim in the Echo.

**Alternative need:** very high. The function should remain, but the library needs several equally strong relation alternatives.

### `relation.direction.02`

```text
Their separate currents share one direction
```

**Signature strength:** high

**Why:** It clearly expresses convergence without fusion, but in a relatively explicit conceptual form.

**Main risk:** adjacency with `shared direction visible` creates a recognisable reprise pattern.

**Alternative need:** high.

### `relation.shore.03`

```text
One shore becomes visible between them
```

**Signature strength:** medium-high

**Why:** The image is more concrete than the direction relation and remains relational without fully explaining itself.

**Main risk:** repeated `visible` language and accumulation with other shore/water blocks.

**Alternative need:** moderate.

### `relation.distance.04`

```text
Distance begins to answer without closing
```

**Signature strength:** very high

**Why:** The phrase is memorable and almost duplicates the wish-entry formulation `distance begins to answer`.

**Main risk:** direct lexical recurrence across multiple roles and Echo positions.

**Alternative need:** very high. This is a prime candidate for controlled rarity or route separation.

## Remainder blocks

### `remainder.passage.01`

```text
{return_word} remains where the passage stays open
```

**Signature strength:** very high

**Why:** It is an effective ending and appeared frequently in early probes. Its cadence and `stays open` closure are strongly recognisable.

**Main risk:** dominating the ending profile of the world.

**Alternative need:** very high.

### `remainder.water.02`

```text
{return_word} waits in the water between them
```

**Signature strength:** medium-high

**Why:** Softer and more image-led than the passage ending. It has a distinctive cadence but is less doctrinal.

**Main risk:** passivity if overused and clustering with water-heavy routes.

**Alternative need:** moderate.

### `remainder.direction.03`

```text
{return_word} keeps the shared direction visible
```

**Signature strength:** high

**Why:** It strongly states the resulting relation and closely echoes `The window keeps two directions visible`.

**Main risk:** syntactic and lexical self-repetition across field and remainder.

**Alternative need:** high.

# 3. Dominant lexical and motif families

The current world deliberately revolves around a coherent motif network. The risk lies not in the presence of these motifs, but in their concentration across too many roles.

## `open` family

Appears in field, wish, relation, remainder, and Echo:

```text
open edge
open frame
interval open
passage stays open
Open distance
```

**Risk:** very high cross-role saturation.

**Recommendation:** retain as a world core, but add alternatives that express permeability without using `open`.

## `trace` family

Appears in atmosphere, same-word proxy, and Echo inheritance:

```text
silver trace
the first trace
the opening trace
```

**Risk:** high in same-word compositions.

**Recommendation:** keep the proxy, but ensure some atmosphere routes contain no trace language.

## `direction` family

Appears in field, response, relation, remainder, and Echo:

```text
two directions visible
another direction
share one direction
shared direction visible
```

**Risk:** very high conceptual repetition.

**Recommendation:** preserve one direction-based relation family, but add relation alternatives based on distance, rhythm, touch, timing, or orientation without the word `direction`.

## `distance` family

Appears in field, wish, relation, and Echo:

```text
distance enter
distance begins to answer
Open distance
rain keeps the distance
```

**Risk:** high because the same phrase can recur across roles.

**Recommendation:** separate exact answering formulations more carefully and add a non-answering distance route.

## `return` family

Appears in movement, response, relation, Echo, and completion semantics:

```text
returning water
tide turns back
stream returns
two returns
returns without undoing
```

**Risk:** medium-high. The family is structurally necessary, but abstract `return(s)` is more conspicuous than image-led return movements.

**Recommendation:** prefer image-led movement in some routes and reserve explicit `return` wording for relation or Echo, not both.

## `visible` family

Appears in field, relation, remainder, and Echo:

```text
two directions visible
shore becomes visible
shared direction visible
keeps {wish_word} visible
```

**Risk:** very high syntactic and conceptual concentration.

**Recommendation:** add outcomes expressed through presence, orientation, nearness, sound, or persistence rather than visibility.

# 4. Dominant syntactic families

## `X keeps Y visible`

Examples:

```text
The window keeps two directions visible
{return_word} keeps the shared direction visible
light keeps {wish_word} visible
```

**Combined signature:** very high

This is one of the clearest assembly-pattern risks in the current library.

## `X remains/waits where Y stays open`

Examples:

```text
{return_word} remains where the passage stays open
one light remains awake
```

**Combined signature:** high

The family is elegant, but repeated endings with `remains`, `stays`, and `open` may sound predesigned.

## `X leaves ...`

Examples:

```text
Rain leaves a silver trace across the floor
Rain leaves room for {wish_word}
```

**Combined signature:** medium-high

This can function as deliberate parallelism, but should not become the ordinary atmosphere-plus-wish pairing.

## `X begins to answer`

Examples:

```text
{wish_word} waits where distance begins to answer
Distance begins to answer without closing
distance begins to answer
```

**Combined signature:** very high

This is currently one phrase family distributed over three structural levels.

## `without undoing`

Examples:

```text
The tide turns back without undoing departure
{wish_word} returns without undoing its path
```

**Combined signature:** high

The ethical idea is valuable. The exact syntax should remain rarer or gain a second expression.

# 5. Relation–remainder pairing review

The current same-word route supports three relation and three remainder families. The matrix below is provisional and describes poetic function, not runtime policy yet.

| Relation | Remainder | Provisional class | Combined signature | Review |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| interval | passage | compatible but marked | very high | Coherent and strong, but already dominant in Series 01 and 02. Needs rarity rather than removal. |
| interval | water | freely compatible | medium-high | Contrast between abstract interval and concrete water softens the machinery. |
| interval | direction | compatible but marked | high | Moves from held space to visible orientation; somewhat conceptual. |
| direction | passage | freely compatible | high | Direction gains an open continuation without lexical duplication. |
| direction | water | freely compatible | medium-high | One of the softer and more balanced combinations. |
| direction | direction | emphatic reprise | very high | Poetically viable, as seen in `Lumen`, but strongly exposes the direction family. Should remain comparatively rare. |
| shore | passage | freely compatible | medium-high | Concrete relation followed by open continuation. |
| shore | water | freely compatible | medium | Highly coherent image family; risk of over-saturation only when movement is also water/shore based. |
| shore | direction | compatible but marked | high | Moves from image to abstraction; may work when surrounding lines remain concrete. |

## Important conclusion

No pair currently requires a categorical ban.

The main need is differentiation between:

```text
ordinary compatibility
marked compatibility
emphatic reprise
```

The `direction + direction` combination should remain available because its repetition can perform a real poetic progression:

```text
sharing
→ becoming visible
```

Its risk comes from frequency, not from inherent failure.

# 6. Highest-priority signature risks

## Tier A — strongest current machine-stamp candidates

```text
Two returns hold one interval open
{return_word} remains where the passage stays open
distance begins to answer
Distance begins to answer without closing
Summer rain softens the threshold
```

These lines are not candidates for removal. They are candidates for:

- stronger static rarity;
- route limitation;
- explicit reprise metadata;
- and equally strong alternatives.

## Tier B — cross-role pattern risks

```text
keeps ... visible
Rain leaves ...
shared direction
open frame / open interval / open passage
without undoing
```

These patterns require family-level review rather than isolated line review.

## Tier C — comparatively flexible support material

```text
A stream returns by its own path
Another current bends seaward through the dark
At the open edge of night
{return_word} waits in the water between them
```

These are still recognisable, but currently provide useful variation rather than dominating it.

# 7. Where alternatives are most urgently needed

The current library should be broadened first in these functions:

1. **Remainder:** alternatives to `passage stays open` and `shared direction visible`.
2. **Relation:** alternatives to `two returns hold one interval open` and direction sharing.
3. **Atmosphere:** at least one route without rain, trace, or threshold.
4. **Answering distance:** a second formulation that does not reuse `begins to answer`.
5. **Visibility outcome:** alternatives based on presence, sound, timing, warmth, or orientation.
6. **Return without erasure:** a second expression without `without undoing`.
7. **Echo line 4:** this is currently the narrowest and most signature-heavy Echo position.

The library does not primarily need more near-synonyms. It needs different poetic operations.

Useful missing operations include:

```text
listening rather than seeing
arrival without closure
temporal delay
shared rhythm
parallel motion
warmth remaining
sound crossing distance
surface change
brief recognition
presence without naming
```

# 8. Complexity-budget consequence

This review does not yet justify a general anti-formula engine.

Most identified risks can first be addressed through readable library data:

- additional blocks;
- clearer route distribution;
- static weights;
- and a small pairwise compatibility map.

New composer logic should only be introduced where the library alone cannot express the poetic distinction clearly.

The preferred order is:

```text
review
→ curate alternatives
→ classify pairings
→ assign static weights
→ test seeded corpora
→ add code only if a remaining need is real
```

This protects both principles:

> Anti-formula quality must be carried by the form of the Nexus, not by a central memory of its copies.

and:

> Poetic depth may require code. Complexity must remain purposeful, local, and readable.

---

# 9. Provisional next step

The next focused task should be a small relation–remainder compatibility and weighting proposal.

It should:

- preserve all nine currently reviewed pairings unless a concrete failure appears;
- classify `direction + direction` as an emphatic reprise;
- reduce the ordinary dominance of `interval + passage`;
- favour several contrasting pairings;
- remain static and local;
- and be represented in readable data before it becomes composer logic.

Before committing those weights, the proposal should be reviewed poetically against the existing probe fixtures.

## Core conclusion

> The current world already has poetic character. Its main risk is not weakness, but the repeated visibility of its strongest formulations.

The goal is therefore not to flatten the voice.

The goal is to give that voice enough different ways to speak.
