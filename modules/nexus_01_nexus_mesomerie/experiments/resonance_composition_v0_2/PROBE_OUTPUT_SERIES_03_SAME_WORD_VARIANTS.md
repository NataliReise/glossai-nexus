## First comparison with Series 02

### Improvements

- Echo line 2 now reflects the selected wish-entry movement through `crosses`, `waits`, `enters`, or `follows`.
- Relation and remainder are no longer forced to one fixed pair.
- The weighted preference makes `the first trace` the ordinary choice while preserving `the opening trace` as a rarer tonal variant.
- The shared word still appears once in the long form and once as the complete fifth Echo line.
- Parallel phrasing remains available.

### New review risks

- `shared direction` may appear in adjacent Echo lines when `relation.direction.02` and `remainder.direction.03` are combined.
- A relation and remainder may be structurally valid yet tonally too similar.
- The movement mapping is curated and transparent, but `enters` is a functional interpretation of `Rain leaves room for ...`; it should remain open to poetic review.
- More combinations increase variety but also increase the need for pairwise compatibility review.

## Immediate review questions

1. Does movement inheritance make the Echo feel more genuinely born from the long form?
2. Is `the first trace` now convincing as the normal proxy, with `the opening trace` as a rarer variant?
3. Which relation and remainder pairings feel complementary, and which feel merely repetitive?
4. Should `relation.direction.02` and `remainder.direction.03` be allowed together, down-weighted, or explicitly curated as an emphatic reprise?
5. Does `waits in the water` preserve enough generative openness, or does it occasionally feel too passive?

No production integration or persistence change is implied by this review matrix.
