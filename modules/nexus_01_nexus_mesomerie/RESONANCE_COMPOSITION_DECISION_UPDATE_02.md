# Resonance Composition Decision Update 02

This document records the second review decision made after
`RESONANCE_COMPOSITION_REDESIGN_V0_2.md` and
`RESONANCE_COMPOSITION_DECISION_UPDATE_01.md`.

It refines the future role of the current Resonance Chamber text elements.

---

## 1. The current texts should move behind the visible poem

**Status: Decided direction**

The currently curated image, scent, movement, response, long-form, and Echo texts are valuable.
They contain much of the tone and relational care developed for the Resonance Chamber.

However, fixed visible formulations create recognisable repetition.
As the number of resonance processes grows, complete lines and familiar text blocks would increasingly reveal the underlying deterministic construction.

The redesigned composer should therefore not normally place the existing formulations directly into the new visible poem.

```text
The old texts should not govern the surface.
They should help govern the possibility space beneath it.
```

The long-form Resonance Artifact should receive newly composed visible language.
The Nexus Echo should then be derived from that newly completed long form.

---

## 2. The old formulations become source material for poetic profiles

**Status: Decided direction**

The connection to the current texts should primarily survive through the profiles derived from them.

The profiles may preserve qualities such as:

- sensory motifs;
- visible objects;
- spatial relations;
- atmosphere;
- temporal depth;
- direction;
- movement qualities;
- relational affordances;
- compatible poetic operators;
- restraint and openness of tone;
- forms of continuation and completion.

For example, a current formulation involving an open window and distant light may help produce a profile containing:

```text
motifs
  window
  light
  stars
  distance

spatial affordances
  threshold
  framing
  inside and outside
  looking outward

operators
  opening
  crossing
  answering
  naming
```

A summer-rain formulation may contribute:

```text
atmospheres
  softening
  awakening
  nearness

traces
  water
  forest
  passing through
  leaving a mark

operators
  carrying
  writing
  touching
```

A returning-tide formulation may contribute:

```text
directions
  return
  convergence
  homeward movement

actions
  carrying back
  meeting
  echoing
```

These examples are conceptual only.
The final profile schema belongs to the poetic prototype and technical design stage.

---

## 3. No required explicit reuse of old wording

**Status: Decided direction**

The redesigned production composer does not need to reproduce the old formulations explicitly.

This means there is no current requirement that:

- an old full line appears;
- an old phrase appears;
- a fixed old stanza appears;
- a canonical old Echo appears;
- a rare production path restores an old formulation verbatim.

The relationship may remain entirely indirect:

```text
old curated formulation
  -> analysed poetic qualities
  -> element profile
  -> compatible operator and motif space
  -> newly composed visible poem
```

The profile may therefore carry the old formulation's atmosphere, direction, or relational logic without carrying its wording.

```text
The formulation may disappear.
Its poetic force may remain.
```

---

## 4. The selected Chamber elements must still matter

**Status: Decided direction**

Moving the old texts behind the visible poem must not make the Chamber selections decorative or interchangeable.

The completed long form should still be shaped by:

- at least one perceivable influence from the selected image profile;
- at least one perceivable influence from the selected scent profile;
- the selected movement and its continuation;
- the resulting completion mode;
- the wish word and return word as the human centre.

A profile influence does not always require the literal naming of its source object.
For example, a starry-window profile might shape threshold, distance, framing, or outward movement even when the word `window` does not appear.

Likewise, a rain profile may shape softness, trace, passage, or nearness without requiring the word `rain`.

The poetic prototype must test whether the selected elements remain perceptible enough without becoming predictable.

---

## 5. Revised relationship between source material and visible outputs

**Status: Decided direction**

```text
current Resonance Chamber texts
  -> historical and stylistic source corpus
  -> poetic profiles and compatibility knowledge

selected image scent movement and responses
  -> active profile combination
  -> bounded operator and motif space

wish word and return word
  -> human relational axis

first local opening
  -> newly composed long-form Resonance Artifact
  -> cryptic Nexus Echo derived from that exact long form
  -> persistent completed local result
```

The visible outputs should therefore be related to the earlier material by lineage rather than quotation.

---

## 6. Consequences for the earlier preservation decision

The earlier concept record proposed several possible future roles for existing language material, including direct reuse as curated fragments or composition material.

This update narrows that direction.

### Preferred role

The current texts should primarily remain as:

- archived historical works;
- stylistic references;
- source material for profile design;
- evidence for why particular motifs, operators, and compatibilities were chosen;
- possible legacy test fixtures where technically necessary.

### No longer preferred as a default production role

They should not normally remain as:

- directly selected production lines;
- mandatory fragments in new long forms;
- rare surprise quotations;
- privileged complete outcomes in the new composer.

This change does not devalue the earlier work.
It changes where that work exerts influence.

```text
The old text becomes foundation rather than facade.
```

---

## 7. Consequences for the long-form-to-Echo linkage rule

The requirement that the Nexus Echo inherit a concrete text fragment continues to refer to the **newly composed long-form Resonance Artifact**.

It does not require the Echo to inherit wording from the old deterministic Chamber texts.

The intended sequence is:

```text
profiles influence the new long form
new long form creates its own visible language
Echo inherits and transforms a trace of that exact language
```

This preserves a perceivable bond between long form and Echo while avoiding repeated old production phrases.

---

## 8. Technical audit implications

The later technical audit should identify separately:

1. files that contain current player-facing Chamber choices;
2. files that contain fixed render-ready formulations;
3. files that contain exact complete long-form or Echo paths;
4. tests that require exact old wording;
5. documentation that treats the old formulations as active production output;
6. material that should inform future profiles;
7. material that should be moved into an intentional archive.

No old file should be deleted before this dependency and lineage review is complete.

The audit should not ask only:

```text
Is this old text still rendered
```

It should also ask:

```text
Which poetic profile was learned from this text
Where will that lineage be documented
```

## 9. Current compact formulation

```text
The Resonance Chamber choices provide forces rather than verses

The old formulations help define those forces
but do not need to appear in the new poem

Their motifs atmosphere movement and relational care
survive through poetic profiles

The first opening creates a new visible long form
The Echo inherits its riddle from that exact long form

The earlier texts remain preserved history
The new poems remain free to become singular
```
